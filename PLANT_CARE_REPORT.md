## 1 · Climate context

**Calabasas is in Sunset Zone 19** — an *interior valley thermal belt*
with weak ocean influence. Summers are hot and dry, winters mild. It's
historically prime citrus country precisely because the summer heat
sweetens the fruit ([Sunset Magazine](https://sunset.com/garden/climate-zones/sunsets-garden-climate-zones)).

USDA Zone 10a means freezes are rare but possible 2–3 nights/year.

### 🥑 Avocado (2 trees — Small Avocado + Avocado Shallow)

**Profile:** ideal moisture **45–65%** (tightest band of any plant here), heat-sensitive, **not** drought-tolerant.

**Why these numbers:** California Avocado Commission says mature trees need ~2 inches of water per week in summer, equivalent to ~45–60 gallons/day per mature tree under drip. Avocados hate drought AND wet feet — they're highly susceptible to Phytophthora root rot if soil stays soggy, but a 7–10 day drought drops leaves and fruit. UC tensiometer research recommends irrigating when 12" tensiometers read 25–30 centibars, which roughly corresponds to ~40% volumetric water content in loam soil.

**This week:**
- **Top priority:** Avocado Shallow at 34% is below the floor — **water tonight, slowly and deeply** to ~50%. Do not flood it.
- **Small Avocado at 39% is borderline** — water Wednesday with the marine layer break.
- **Before Friday evening:** both should be at ~55% to weather the heatwave. With 0.30"/day ET₀ during peak heat, an avocado can drop 5–8% in a day.

**Critical heat-wave advice:** avocados sunburn easily. If trunks/branches are exposed, paint the south-facing trunk with diluted (50/50) white latex paint, or use shade cloth on the 94°F days.

### 🌸 Camellia (2 plants — both labeled "Camelia")

**Profile:** ideal moisture **35–50%** (note: HIGHER floor than other broadleaf, but LOWER ceiling than I had originally), heat-sensitive.

**Why these numbers:** UC Master Gardeners are emphatic — *"more commonly damaged by over-watering than under-watering."* Deep water once a week in summer. Established camellias can tolerate infrequent irrigation. **Bud drop is the #1 sign of summer water stress.** They also need protection from hot afternoon sun and drying winds — Calabasas inland heat + 10% RH can scorch leaves fast.

**This week:**
- Back Yard Camelia (33%): **water tonight**, then leave alone through the marine layer.
- Side Yards Camelia (33%): same.
- **Before Friday:** make sure both are at ~45%. Then mulch 2–3" around the root zone if not already done.
- **During heatwave:** if leaves curl, shade cloth them — that's a sign of UV + heat damage, not necessarily water stress.
